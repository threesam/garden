# raw thoughts — self.md session

unfiltered stories from the conversation that shaped self.md. kept for history.

## benny

every night after our shift at the bar, we would go to his house, smoke a joint, and talk for hours into the night about our life, our dreams. he was always full of hustle, he had a hard life, was clean for over a decade and built up successful businesses brick by brick. he gave everyone a chance, was an incredible musician and could play anything, got excited about new bands, supported his friends' dreams, listened to an album intently before judging. if he loved you he was all in, would turn over mountains to help get you where you needed to be, was the funniest human i've ever hung out with, and the most generous.

## the open mic

the open mic was home because of who Benny was, he infused it with his philosphy on life/love, it was fair, everyone got their chance (not that he didn't shift things around to make sure the show was engaging for everyone), the room was usually packed because he was excellent at promoting, snapping picks, making everyone feel like they're part of the magic even if they are only observing. i remember my first time on stage being great, i was solid at performing solo but i started playing my original song and about halfway through someone jumped on cahon, i think benny hoped on bass and figured out my song in real time and by the end a saxophone was also blaring having figured out how to riff on the final chorus. i didn;t realize i could feel that alive and i was a mainstay week after week for years (usually leading to the aforementioned smoke with benny and often friends after).

## meeting deana

the first thing i said to my future wife online was "hey, blondie" and while i was definitely holding court with tons of women after having finally found my stride in a thriving music community, after i started talking to her i didnt want to talk to anyone else. i had invited her to the open mic (very public easy) and she showed up with her friend (both of them wearing wigs lol) not that i would have necessarily noticed anyway in the dark room, she wanted to see if i was a player, Benny the shutterbug that he was snapped a pic showing that we were 3 feet apart at one point and i didn't even notice. the next week we hung out offically, when we were bartending and Deana brought two of her friends with her, so i was basically on a date with 3 women out first time, they felt comfortable enough to come back to Benny's house after the shift (our ritual) and listen as benny and me brought them down into our basement studio and built some loops and sang songs into the night, her friends were pretty and fun, but i knew i loved this girl from basically the first time i saw her, she was funny and had this cold Russian persona that i needed to crack, it didn't take long and we became inseparable, then officially began dating and never looked back, her parents kept asking when we'd get married and we had a quaker wedding a month before Phoenix was born.

## benny's death

benny gave me all the leway to become a dad, hooked me up with easy money, but secretly he was struggling, he had relapsed shortly after Phoenix was born, and i only saw him a few times before he was gone. i remember meeting him at his house and i he didn't need to tell me he relapsed, it was obvious, he was a shell of the person who had become my best friend, though underneath the struggle i could still see him in there, he was gone 5 months into me being a dad. when i found out he had died, it was a unexpected but not surprising. i had lost my role model, friend, business partner, the god father to my kid. he didn't have much family and the ones he did have weren't as familiar with the person i knew him as, they were more familliar with the addict he had been and returned to. my business relationship allowed me to keep many of his namesakes, his art and his instruments, specifically his acoustic guitars, the Taylor's that he would put out every open mic so that even if people didn't have the means to own a guitar, they had the option to play them, his legacy came to me.

## learning to code

i walked outside with the kids for 4 hours every day, even when it was raining so i could listen to podcasts and courses, it was the only way i could absorb knowledge while they were awake, i wore holes in the bottoms of my flip flops, my father in thought i was a "forever student" and was asking his friends if i could work with them, when i eventually got a job he apologized and our relationship has never been better, he just wanted to know that i would be able to support his daughter and grandkids.

## first day at work

first day at work i was asked to decrypt a 30k email list, i had no idea how to do it, what it was encrypted with, asked around and no-one actually knew how to help me, eventually we figured out it was base64 encrypted and i wrote a script that decrypted it and felt the biggest rush, addicted.

## money

when azalea was on the way i started reading books, a lot, finished 40 my first year, personal development, learned about masculinity, responsibility, and money. one of the main things i internalized was this idea that money is technology, it's energy, and if treated like a friend it will be a friend. if i say shit like "my friend" is the root of all evil, that friend is going to want nothing to do with me. i remember geting a job and being able to take my wife out to a nice dinner and feeling guilty, like i didn't deserve it, like it betrayed my upbringing that was full of love but not of extravagence. i remember being able to give the homeless i encountered a 5 dollor bill without blinking, and remembered the generosity of Benny who gave away damn near everything he made and recieved it all back tenfold.

## ai shift

the first thing that felt really different was being able to effectively talk to our data, we had lost access to some important dashboards and through CLIs i was able to mainline directly into our analytics provider and extract the lost data and build a dashboard that was more complete and useful than the one we lost -- it took me about an hour. all of things i talked about wanting to do in the future but knew i didnt really have time to get to where able to be done in a day or less, after two weeks i realized i had cleared about a years' worth of nice-to-haves without any deteriment to my current workload.

## growing up in trenton

my parents got a house in a cute area outside of a nursing home, safe, but the city didn't grow, it just got more and more impoverished, more corrupted, i was in public school there until 5th grade, 6th grade was when i got into private, i would have had to do public in Trenton and that could have been rough, when a city dies, it does so slowly. and i lived mostly inside my house, reclusive. it wasnt all bad, i remember sitting with my dad waiting for the bus to come pick me up to go Princeton Day, it was weird to go to this perfectly manacured private school and thne get picked up by my dad in the intercity ghetto that i grew up in, culture shock that i got used to.

## valerie

her name was Valerie and she eventually dated Benny though my friendship outlasted their romance though they remained friends throughout -- he had an uncanny ability to turn ex lovers into friends.

## six to midnight

it was the studio we set up together, it was mostly in an effort to make "cool shit", there were some standout events, we took over an outdoor actually beautiful amphitheater in Trenton, got permits and put on a super cool event with local music (super fun) and snacks/refreshments, very community driven and even filmed a mini doc about it. we also did a corporate event that was a flex for us. we wore suits, and kept the room warm even in a corporate environment (i later did the same event a year later, after he had died and it was not the same), we did a wedding for one of his ex girlfriends outdoors in a farm and transformed the area with lights and music. Deana even played DJ for the night and got the room grooving hardddd. Sidenote: my wife was affected heavily by Bennys loss, we were all pseudo family. another event was a private party at someones house in Trenton where we supplied alcolhol, music, vibes and it was one of the most electric rooms i have ever been in (even though teh people we hosted it for got kind of too drunk and had a public meltdown). one communtiy thing from before we started our company was Benny took to this thing called the Hive, it was a warehouse that we transformed once a month into a stage where local bands (who were all amazing) played, artists live painted murals/art, and community grew as it went for 24 hours once a month. this, along with the open mic, was what changed my perspective on Trenton, and just how powerful the right people, even in a shitty place, can turn a city around that is otherwise dying.

## meeting the in-laws

i remember the first time I met Deana's dad, he came earlier than she thought he would, Deana, her firend and me were just hanging out and this 6'5 tower of a human walked through teh door and gracefully shook my hand. it was tense but he was also ok, i knew i would win him over eventually. he is a pastor for a Russian Baptist church and presides over all of north america. me not being a christian and not married to his daughter was an obvious tension point, considering he allowed me to live with them is really interesting when looking at after the fact. he showed significant grace when he didn't have to. i'm also easy to live with considering i'm respectful, cook very well and clean. even though we didn't speak the same language i could navigate well non-verbally though i know there was tension and comments for deana.

## deana during the hard years

deana had to weather a shit ton of flak from her parents, she had to return to work after 3 months (only half paid) for both phoenix and then juniper. she wanted to breastfeed but pumping is hard during work, the stress mounts when you're the breadwinner and the supply dries up, because she wasn't making much money we had enough to survive but not enough to thrive, we had to live her parents, and with two kids, us, her parents, and a dog (that the parents did not necessarily approve of but did tolerate) it was cramped. when juniper was 5 months olf we got accepted into rent-controlled housing (so did her parents) and that was the inflection point of our relationship building better, and the tension starting to lessen. the dar after juniper turned one, i started my first job. deana tried to work too but after 3 weeks i convinced her to stay home -- she resisted hard, her identity being wrapped up in working since before she knew me, it was her only form of freedom previously, and only with time did she realize that she wasn't going to be unfree after she stopped working (i mean parenting is way harder than working but still).

## the career

my first job was at Rhone where Isaac, my first manager, took a chance on me and was one of the most kind people i had ever met while being in love with the work he was doing. he changed my life, and he's not with us anymore which is a travesty, more loss i've had to deal with. but after a year and half i switched roles and went to Made In Cookware, got a sizable bump in salary that helped support my growing family, and met Dan (our lead engineer) who was also self-taught like me and is brilliant. i learned so much from watching him work, he eventually left and i stepped up to lead thereafter and have grown into the role and beyond.

## brookstone to recording

Brookstone downsized and even though my store was one of the only ones profitable, it got closed down and i was laid off, i used my severence money to buy music equipment and started recording myself. i was looking for people to record and that's when i talked to valerie who indicated she might want to record some music but instead invited me to the open mic and said i needed to meet some guy (benny).

## mom

my mom helped me through school, was super nurturing and even though the city we lived in became more and more bleak, she remained a soft and consistence presence.
